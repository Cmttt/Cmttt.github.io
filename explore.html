Average dribbles vs. Average shot distance

This visualization shows the average dribbles and average shot distance for players shooting. Average dribbles was compared to average shot distance as average dribbles and time held are strongly correlated and average shot distance and closest defender are correlated as well. This was not used in our predictive modeling but is a good way to see the players that tend to be "chuckers" or ballhogs. For example, Mo Williams is on the far right of the graph, shooting long shots while holding the ball for many dribbles per possession.

Best Shot Contests

This graph displays players that were the closest defender for the largest percentage of misses. This was not used for the prediction model as well, but it gives a good idea on who the best shot contesters were in the 2014-2015 season.

Makes vs. No Makes - Attributes

This visualization was made to determine which attributes should be picked when trying to determine if a shot is made or not. We looked at the numerical variables included in the dataset including: average dribbles, average time on shot clock, average touch time, average close defender distance and average shot distance.

NBA Team Win Rate

This visualization displays each team's win rate from 1986 to 2018. San Antonio Spurs is the most stable team during the past 20 years.

Significant Factors for Win Rate

This visualization compares different pairs of factors for win rate of a team, including field goal percentage and three point percentage, assist and turnover, steal and foul, points and points lose. For example, teams with more assists and less turnovers, such as Oklahoma City Thunder, tend to win more games.
